Wednesday, Shadow or Substance?

Colossians · Week 10, Day 5
March 4, 2026
✦ ☩ ✦
📌 TL;DR
A shadow only makes sense when you know what is casting it. Once you are standing in the light of the real thing, you stop staring at the outline on the ground.
  • ☩Colossians 2:16–19 addresses Jewish-Christian practices being pushed on the Gentile church in Colossae: meal and drink offerings, festival calendars, new moon observances, and ceremonial sabbaths. Paul's verdict: these were shadows. Christ is the substance.
  • ☩Paul's word "therefore" in verse 16 is a hinge: he is drawing a conclusion from everything he just established about the Cross cancelling our sin-debt. If the debt is gone, no one has any standing to judge you over the ceremonies that pointed to its cancellation.
  • ☩The sequence "festival, new moon, sabbaths" mirrors an Old Testament ceremonial calendar pattern found in Hosea 2:11 and Leviticus 23, pointing clearly to the annual ceremonial sabbaths, not the seventh-day Sabbath instituted at Creation before sin ever existed.
  • ☩The seventh-day Sabbath was created in Eden, before sin, before sacrifice, before the ceremonial system. It was never a shadow pointing forward to the Cross. It is a memorial pointing backward to Creation, and forward to the Creator who redeems what he made.
🗺️ Historical & Cultural Context
A Mixed Church Under Mixed Pressure

Colossae was a predominantly Gentile city, but the church there included Jewish believers who had carried their religious practices with them into the Christian community. Scholars still debate the precise makeup of the Colossian heresy, but the letter itself makes one thing clear: someone in or near the church was pushing Jewish ceremonial observances on Gentile members who had no prior obligation to them. The practices in Colossians 2:16, food and drink offerings, festival calendars, new moons, ceremonial sabbaths, are precisely the practices that set Jewish worship apart from the surrounding Gentile world.

This same tension had surfaced elsewhere. Paul addressed it in Galatia (Galatians 4:10), and the Jerusalem Council had already ruled on it in Acts 15. The issue in Colossae is the same: Jewish converts, sincere in their faith, treating the ceremonial calendar as a requirement for all believers. Paul's response is not to attack the calendar's history or deny its purpose. He simply points to what it always pointed to: Christ. The shadow served its function. The substance has arrived. You don't keep staring at the shadow once you are standing in the light.

📖 Into the Word

Today's anchor passage is Colossians 2:16–19, where Paul applies the logic of the Cross directly to the question of Jewish ceremonial observances. His argument hinges on verse 17's distinction between skia (shadow) and soma (body or substance): the ceremonial calendar was a shadow cast by the coming reality of Christ. To understand which sabbaths Paul has in mind, the study draws on Hosea 2:11 and Leviticus 23, which both use the same ceremonial sequence Paul is referencing. Paul then shifts in verses 18–19 to address a second problem: religious performance and angel veneration that had disconnected some from Christ himself, the head from whom the whole body grows.

Colossians 2:16–17
☩ Open in Bible App
"So let no one judge you in food or in drink, or regarding a festival or a new moon or sabbaths, which are a shadow of things to come, but the substance is of Christ."
New King James Version · NKJV
The opening word "therefore" (oun in Greek, rendered "so" in the NKJV) is critical. Paul is drawing a direct conclusion from what he just established in verses 13–15: the sin-debt cancelled at the Cross, the ceremonial system fulfilled in Christ. Because all of that is true, no one has standing to judge you over the observance of ceremonies that existed to point toward it. The Greek word skia means shadow, silhouette, or shade: an outline cast by something real that is approaching. The Greek word soma means body, or in this context, the substance and reality itself. Paul's point is not that the shadow was worthless, shadows are real and useful. They tell you something real is coming. But once the real thing arrives, you orient to the substance, not the outline. "Food and drink" here refers to the meal and drink offerings brought to the temple, not to dietary laws in general. The "festival, new moon, sabbaths" sequence appears verbatim in the Old Testament ceremonial calendar, see Hosea 2:11 and Leviticus 23:11, 24, 32.
Hosea 2:11
☩ Open in Bible App
"I will put an end to all her celebrations: her feasts, her new moons, her sabbaths, and all her festivals."
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
Hosea's prophecy of judgment against Israel uses the same triadic sequence Paul cites in Colossians 2:16: feasts, new moons, sabbaths. In Hosea, these are called "her sabbaths": possessives that distinguish them from the seventh-day Sabbath, which God consistently calls "my Sabbath" or "the Sabbath of the Lord" throughout Scripture (see Exodus 20:10; Leviticus 23:3). The ceremonial sabbaths belonged to the Jewish festival system and were tied to the sacrificial calendar. The weekly Sabbath belonged to God and was instituted for all humanity at Creation, before Israel existed as a people.
Leviticus 23:11, 24, 32
☩ Open in Bible App
"He is to wave the sheaf before the Lord so that it will be accepted on your behalf; the priest is to wave it on the day after the Sabbath... Say to the Israelites: In the seventh month, on the first day of the month, you are to have a day of complete rest, a sacred assembly commemorated with trumpet blasts... It will be a Sabbath of complete rest for you, and you must practice self-denial. You are to practice this self-denial from the evening of the ninth to the evening of the tenth day of the month."
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
Leviticus 23 is the definitive inventory of the Israelite ceremonial calendar, and it uses the term "sabbath" for annual observances entirely separate from the weekly seventh day. The Feast of Firstfruits (v. 11), the Feast of Trumpets (v. 24), and the Day of Atonement (v. 32) are all called sabbaths: they fall on different days of the week each year, tied to the agricultural and sacrificial calendar rather than the seven-day creation cycle. These are the ceremonial sabbaths Paul has in mind in Colossians 2:16: the ones whose shadows pointed directly to Christ's sacrifice and priestly ministry, and which met their fulfillment when he died (see 1 Corinthians 5:7 and 1 Corinthians 15:23).
1 Corinthians 5:7
☩ Open in Bible App
"For Christ our Passover lamb has been sacrificed."
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
Paul's shorthand identification of Christ as the Passover lamb is exactly the kind of fulfillment language that explains why the ceremonial shadows are no longer in force. The Passover was a shadow: a lamb slain, blood applied to the doorpost, death passing over those covered by it. It was always pointing toward something it could not itself accomplish. When "Christ our Passover" was sacrificed, the shadow met the substance. Continuing to keep the Passover as an obligation after the Cross would be like insisting the sign still says "bridge ahead" after you are already across the bridge.
1 Corinthians 15:23
☩ Open in Bible App
"But each in his own order: Christ the firstfruits, then at his coming those who belong to Christ."
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
The Feast of Firstfruits, one of the ceremonial sabbaths in Leviticus 23, was the offering of the first sheaf of harvest, waved before God as a pledge that the rest of the harvest would follow. Paul identifies Christ's resurrection as the fulfillment of this feast: he is the firstfruits, and the resurrection of those who belong to him is the full harvest that follows. When Christ rose on the day after the Sabbath (Leviticus 23:11), he fulfilled the Feast of Firstfruits at the precise moment the calendar prescribed. The shadow and the substance met at the exact right time.
Colossians 2:18–19
☩ Open in Bible App
"Let no one disqualify you, insisting on ascetic practices and the worship of angels, claiming access to a visionary realm and inflated without cause by his fleshly mind, and not holding on to the head, from whom the whole body, nourished and held together by its ligaments and tendons, grows with growth from God."
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
Paul now addresses a second, more troubling dimension of the Colossian problem: religious performance and the veneration of angels. The Dead Sea Scrolls from Qumran confirm that angelic worship featured in some Jewish communities of this period, angels as intermediaries, as objects of elaborate liturgy, as gatekeepers of spiritual access. Jesus had criticized religious pretension among the Jewish leaders of his day (see Matthew 6:1, 5, 7, 16), and Paul sees the same pattern here. The phrase "not holding on to the head" is the diagnostic. Whatever spiritual system disconnects you from direct, personal connection to Christ as the head of the body is not depth, it is drift. The body grows from the head. Cut the head off and the body doesn't become more spiritual. It dies.
Matthew 6:1, 5, 7, 16
☩ Open in Bible App
"Be careful not to practice your righteousness in front of others to be seen by them... Whenever you pray, you must not be like the hypocrites, because they love to pray standing in the synagogues and on the street corners to be seen by people... When you pray, don't babble like the Gentiles, since they imagine they'll be heard for their many words... Whenever you fast, don't be gloomy-faced like the hypocrites. For they make their faces unattractive so that their fasting is obvious to people."
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
Jesus's Sermon on the Mount critique of religious performance is the backdrop for Paul's warning in Colossians 2:18. Giving, praying, fasting, none of these are wrong. Jesus taught and practiced all three. The problem is when the performance of them becomes the point: when the display of religious humility is itself a form of pride, when elaborate spiritual practice is really about being seen as spiritually advanced. The Colossian teachers who were promoting ascetic practices and angel worship fit this profile precisely: their system required a level of special access and spiritual sophistication that, not coincidentally, only they could provide.
Genesis 2:2–3
☩ Open in Bible App
"On the seventh day God had completed his work that he had done, and he rested on the seventh day from all his work that he had done. God blessed the seventh day and declared it holy, for on it he rested from all his work of creation."
Christian Standard Bible · CSB
This is the foundation passage for understanding why the seventh-day Sabbath stands in a completely different category from the ceremonial sabbaths Paul addresses in Colossians 2:16. The Sabbath was instituted at Creation, before sin, before sacrifice, before the entire ceremonial system existed. It is not a shadow pointing forward to something coming, it is a memorial pointing backward to something God already completed. It was given to humanity, not to Israel specifically (Mark 2:27: "The Sabbath was made for man"). And it was enshrined in the Ten Commandments, written by God's own hand in stone, not in Moses's book, as a permanent, moral declaration of who God is and what he completed. The ceremonial sacrifices of the sanctuary were adopted long after Eden, because of sin. The seventh-day Sabbath predates all of it.
🎯 Street Level
The Difference Between a Trailer and the Movie

A movie trailer exists for one purpose: to point you toward something coming. It gives you images, sounds, and a taste of the story: enough to make you want to see the real thing. Nobody watches the trailer on repeat after they have seen the film. You don't frame the poster of "coming soon" after the movie is out. The trailer was always pointing forward, and once the movie arrives, you go to the movie.

The ceremonial calendar of Israel was a theological trailer. The Passover lamb pointed to the Lamb of God who takes away the sin of the world. The firstfruits offering pointed to the firstfruits of the resurrection. The Day of Atonement pointed to the one act of atonement that would accomplish what all the annual sacrifices could only gesture toward. When Christ arrived and died and rose, the trailer met the film. Paul's point in Colossians 2:16–17 is simply this: now that you have the film, don't let anyone condemn you for not watching the trailer anymore. The shadow was always meant to give way to the substance. That is not loss. That is arrival.

🌍 Today's World, Ancient Truth
The Resurgence of Hebrew Roots: When the Trailer Comes Back

One of the more striking religious trends of the past decade is the rapid growth of the Hebrew Roots Movement: a loosely organized network of Christians who have concluded that the ceremonial law of Moses, including the full Jewish festival calendar, remains binding on all believers. Estimates suggest the movement has grown significantly since 2020, with many former evangelicals drawn in through YouTube channels and podcasts emphasizing the "Jewish roots" of the faith. The appeal is real: a desire for depth, historical grounding, and a sense that modern Christianity has lost something essential.

Paul addressed exactly this impulse in Colossians 2. The Jewish believers pushing the ceremonial calendar on Gentile converts were not malicious: they were sincere. The feasts were beautiful. The calendar was richly meaningful. The problem was not the shadow itself; the problem was treating the shadow as the destination after the substance had arrived. When the Hebrew Roots Movement insists that keeping the Passover, the Feast of Tabernacles, or the ceremonial sabbaths is required for full obedience, it is, however unintentionally, doing what Paul warned against: letting the shadow judge you rather than letting Christ free you. The substance is of Christ. The trailer pointed to the film. The film has arrived.

✝️ The Christ Connection

Paul's word for the substance in verse 17 is soma: body. "The body is of Christ." This is not accidental. Christ is not just the reality to which the shadows pointed; he is the embodiment of everything the entire ceremonial system was designed to communicate. He is the Passover lamb, the firstfruits of resurrection, the high priest who entered the true sanctuary once and for all, the Day of Atonement completed in a single act (Hebrews 9:12). Every feast, every offering, every ceremonial sabbath was a different angle on the same story: a holy God making a way for broken people to come home. Christ is that way, fully, finally, and completely.

And then verse 19: "not holding on to the head, from whom the whole body grows with growth from God." This is the ultimate Christ connection in today's passage. All genuine spiritual growth, the kind that is actually from God, flows from connection to Christ as the head. Not through angel intermediaries. Not through elaborate ceremonial performance. Not through spiritual gatekeepers with exclusive access to visionary realms. Directly. Personally. Through him. The body grows from the head. There is no other source.

✅ Personal Application Challenge
Paul's question cuts both directions today. Are you holding onto shadows: practices, rituals, or religious performances that have become the point rather than pointing to Christ? Or have you swung the other way and discarded something that was never a shadow at all? Take five minutes today to honestly ask: what in my spiritual life is genuinely connected to Christ as the head, and what is performance for its own sake?
🔁 Connection to Yesterday

Yesterday's study established that the sin-debt recorded against us, our personal cheirographon, was nailed to the cross and cancelled. Today Paul draws the direct conclusion from that cancellation: because the debt is gone, no one has any basis to judge you over the ceremonial system that existed to point toward its payment. The shadows served their purpose precisely because the substance was coming. Now that the substance has arrived, the shadows have fulfilled their function.

Together these two studies form a single coherent argument running through Colossians 2:11–19: Christ accomplished everything the ceremonial system was designed to point toward, and therefore no human system: ceremonial calendar, angel hierarchy, ascetic practice, or philosophical framework, has any standing to add to what he completed or to judge those who are fully found in him.

🙏 Closing Prayer

Lord, thank you for the shadows, for the feasts, the sacrifices, the calendar that told the story of what was coming before we could see it clearly. And thank you that the substance arrived. That the Passover lamb was slain. That the firstfruits rose. That the atonement was made, once, completely, finally. Help me stop staring at shadows and start living in the light of the real thing. And help me hold on to you as the head, the one from whom all genuine growth actually comes. Not through performance. Not through elaborate systems. Just you. Amen.
